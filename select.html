Mushi-Ken

According to the World Rock Paper Scissors Association, The earliest Japanese “Rock-Paper-Scissors” game was known as “mushi-ken”, which was imported directly from China. In “Mushi-ken” the “frog” (represented by the thumb) is beaten by the “slug” (represented by the little finger), which in turn is beaten by the “snake” (represented by the index finger), which is beaten by the “frog”. Although this game was imported from China, the Japanese version differs in the animals represented.

Rochambeau

Rumor has it that this game was named after Comte de Roshambeau, A French general who helped to defeat the British at Yorktown during the American Revolutionary War. This is classic Rock-Paper-Scissors, played from childhood through adulthood. This is the game you know and love. The rules are simple. Paper covers rock, Rock breaks scissors and scissors cuts paper.

Wizard Fight

This game is inspired by the best selling Harry Potter series by J.K. Rowling. In this version of the game, the player uses spells instead of tools. The rules for this game were modified from a game outlined by Samma Fagan. The Confundus spell beats the Protego spell, the Protego spell beats the Flipendo spell and the Flipendo spell beats the Confundus spell.
